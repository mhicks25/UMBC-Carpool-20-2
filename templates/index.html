Why UMBC Carpool

At University of Maryland, Baltimore County, we understand the importance of getting to campus because we are students ourselves. That's why we, as student developers, have created UMBC carpool app tailored specifically for our community.

Driven by our firsthand experience as commuters, we recognize the unique challenges faced by our peers. With 60% of students relying on commuting, we understand the necessity of reliable, affordable, and sustainable transportation solutions.

Our mission is to harness the collective power of our student body through our carpool app. By connecting drivers and passengers within our university community, we empower students to support each other in their journey to and from campus.

As fellow students, we are committed to ensuring that every member of our community has access to transportation, enabling them to pursue their academic goals without barriers. Together, we believe in creating a campus environment where every student can thrive.

Join us in revolutionizing the way we commute, driven by students, for students.

Sustainability

Our commitment to sustainability aligns seamlessly with our university's mission to reduce greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, particularly those stemming from transportation. According to data from UMBC's sustainability page, transportation activities contribute significantly to our campus's carbon footprint, accounting for over 44% of GHG emissions.

In the fall year of 2018 alone, UMBC generated an estimated 31,400 metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent (MTeCO2) from transportation sources. Commuting by faculty, staff, and students represents the largest contributor, comprising 68% of these emissions.

By encouraging convenient access to carpooling through our app, we directly address this challenge, providing a sustainable alternative that reduces the environmental impact of commuting. Through our collective efforts, we aim to contribute to UMBC's broader sustainability goals, fostering a greener campus and a brighter future for all.
